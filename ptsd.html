PTSD/Post Tramatic Stress Disorder


What's Post Tramatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)

PTSD is a real disorder that develops when a person has experienced or witnessed a scary, shocking, terrifying, or dangerous event.


PTSD In The World

PTSD affects a significant portion of the global population. Estimates suggest that approximately 3.9% of individuals worldwide experience PTSD during their lifetime.


Symtom Of PTSD

Some PTSD symptoms include:

  • Nightmares
  • Flashback
  • Avoiding Reminders Of The Event
  • Forgetting Important Aspects Of The Traumatic Event
  • Unable To Experience Positive Emotions
  • Irritability And Angry Outburst
  • Negative Thoughts About Yourself Or Other

What Cause PTSD

  1. Violence: assault, abuse, or war.
  2. Accidents: car crashes or naturaldisasters.
  3. Loss: losing a loved one or seeing death.
  4. Childhood trauma: abuse or neglect
  5. Stressful jobs: frist responders, soldiers, or caregiver
  6. No support: lack of help after the trauma


How To Reduce It

  1. Seek Professional Help: work with a therapist specializing in PTSD,such as one trained in Cognitive Behavioral Therapy(CBT)or Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing(EMDR)
  2. Practice Relaxation: Techniques:deep breathing deep breathing,mindfulness, or meditation can help calm the body and mind
  3. Stay Active:regular: physical activity,like walking or yoga,can reduce stress and improve mood
  4. Build A Support System:Talk to trusted friend,family,or support groups about your feelings
  5. Limit Triggers: identify and avoid situation, media ,or environment that exacerbate symptoms
