Borderline Personality Disorder(BPD)


What's Borderline Personality Disorder

Borderline personality disorder is a mental disorder chharacterised by unstable mood,behavior and relationships. the cause of borderline personality disorder isn't well understood.


Borderline Personalty Disorder In The World

A research shows that around 1 in 100 people live with BTD worldwide and it is most common in early adulthood

Symtom Of Borderline Personality Disorder

Symtoms can range from manageableto very severe and can include any combination of the following

  • Fear Of Abandonment
  • Unstable Intense Relationships
  • Unstable Self-Image Or Sense Of Self
  • Rapid Mood Changes
  • Impulsive And Dangerous Behavior
  • Persistent Feeling Of Emptiness
  • Anger Management Issues
  • Temporary Paranoid Thoughts

What Cause Borderline Personality Disorder

  1. Brain differences: People with BPD might have issues with brain areas that control emotions, leading to intense feelings and impulsive behavior.
  2. Unstable environment: Growing up in a chaotic or invalidating family situation might also contribute.
  3. Trauma: Experiencing abuse, neglect, or unstable relationships as a child can increase the risk.
  4. Family history: If a close relative has BPD, you're more likely to develop it.

Some people are naturally more sensitive or reactive, making it harder for them to manage emotions, which can set the stage for BPD if other factors are present.

How To reduce It

  1. Schema Therapy: Addresses deep-rooted emotional patterns and helps build healthier coping mechanisms.
  2. Mindfulness: Practice staying present and focused on the moment to reduce emotional intensity.
  3. Journaling: Write down your feelings to better understand and process them.
  4. Eat a balanced diet: A healthy diet supports brain health and emotional stability.
  5. Avoid alcohol and drugs: These can worsen impulsive behaviors and emotional instability.
